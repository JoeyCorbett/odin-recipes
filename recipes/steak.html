Filet Mignon

Description

Filet mignon is the smaller tip of tenderloin and one of the most expensive cuts of steak, due to its prized texture and that it's only about 2% of the total animal.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil
  • (6-oz.) filet mignon
  • Kosher Salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp. roughly chopped rosemary

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400Â°. In a large skillet over medium-high heat, heat oil. Season steak with salt and pepper on both sides
  2. When oil is just about to smoke, add steak. Cook until very seared, about 5 minutes, then flip and add butter and rosemary.
  3. Baste with butter and cook another 3 to 5
  4. Transfer skillet to oven and cook until cooked to your liking, about 5 minutes for medium
  5. If you are within 10 degrees, you may need even less time. If your steak isn't done after 5 minutes, check every couple of minutes so you don't risk over cooking.
  6. Remove from pan and let rest 5 minutes before slicing.
